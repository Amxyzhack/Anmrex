Government Licenses

Regulatory Licenses & Authorizations

Anmrex's regulatory standing is built on verifiable government-issued licenses and ongoing compliance with applicable financial regulatory frameworks.

🏛️

Money Services Business (MSB) License

U.S. Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN)

The FinCEN MSB license establishes Anmrex as a registered money services business under U.S. federal law. This license requires full compliance with the Bank Secrecy Act, including AML program implementation, suspicious activity reporting, and record-keeping obligations. Anmrex obtained this license in July 2022 and successfully passed renewal in July 2025, confirming continued compliance with FinCEN's regulatory requirements.

International Certifications

Security & Data Protection Standards

Beyond government licensing, Anmrex has achieved internationally recognized technical and operational certifications that validate the quality of its internal systems.

🏅

ISO/IEC 27001 — Information Security

The global standard for information security management. ISO 27001 certification verifies that Anmrex has implemented a systematic, risk-based approach to managing information security — covering people, processes, and technology. Achieved December 2024, subject to annual review.

📊

SOC 2 Type II — Service Controls

The SOC 2 certification independently verifies Anmrex's internal controls related to security, availability, and confidentiality. Type II certification is more rigorous than Type I — it assesses controls over an extended observation period, not just at a single point in time. Achieved December 2024.

🇪🇺

GDPR Compliance

Full compliance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation. Anmrex completed a formal GDPR data compliance review in May 2025, ensuring all user data collection, storage, processing, and usage fully conforms to EU data protection law — applicable to all users regardless of location.

Compliance Programs

AML, KYC & Sanctions Screening

Anmrex operates a full-spectrum compliance program that goes beyond minimum regulatory requirements.

🪪

Customer Identification (KYC)

All users undergo identity verification before accessing the platform. The KYC process includes document verification, liveness detection, and risk-based due diligence, with enhanced procedures applied to high-risk or high-volume accounts. Implemented fully since September 2021.

🔍

Anti-Money Laundering (AML)

Anmrex's AML program includes transaction monitoring, suspicious activity detection, and mandatory reporting obligations. Machine learning models continuously analyze transaction patterns for potential laundering behavior, with human review escalation and regulatory reporting as required.

🚫

Sanctions List Screening

All users and transactions are screened against international sanctions lists including OFAC, UN, and EU consolidated lists. Screening is performed at onboarding and on an ongoing basis, ensuring Anmrex has no exposure to sanctioned individuals, entities, or jurisdictions.

⛓️

On-Chain Compliance Monitoring

Powered by Chainalysis and Elliptic, Anmrex monitors the on-chain history of all deposited funds. Address risk scoring, fund source analysis, and cross-platform tracking enable proactive identification of high-risk transactions before they impact platform operations.

🌐

Cross-Jurisdictional Compatibility

Anmrex's compliance framework is designed for multi-jurisdictional operation. The legal team continuously monitors evolving regulatory environments globally, ensuring the platform remains compliant as frameworks change. Cross-jurisdictional research presented at Dubai CFC in March 2025.

📁

Regulatory Reporting

Anmrex meets all applicable regulatory reporting obligations, including Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) to FinCEN, Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs), and other jurisdiction-specific filings. All reporting is processed through the Compliance and Risk Management Department established in 2021.
